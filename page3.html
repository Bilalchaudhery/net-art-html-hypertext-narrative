PAGE THREE: LOOP

The roses finally fall apart into pure pattern. No petals, just pixels shaped like petals. No perfume, just the ghost of how it felt.

“I kept your night for you,” the garden says.
“I saved it and saved it and saved it.
Now the file only remembers roses and light.”

You realize the memory is more aesthetic than accurate. It is not lying, it is just wearing too much glitter. The story does not end; it just reloads.

Can you believe this garden, and can you believe this loop, and can you believe you are about to start over like nothing glitched at all?
