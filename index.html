ROSES IN THE STATIC

Tonight the internet opens like a velvet curtain. Somewhere between a perfume ad and a corrupted file, there is a rose garden made of light, pixels, and half-remembered nights.

You swear you’ve been here before: champagne bubbles, sequins on the floor, a flash photo of you in a cracked mirror. But the page stutters. Letters repeat. Time feels off by one frame.

This happens: you click on a flower, and then this happens: the screen fills with roses, and then this happens: the memory forgets parts of you and saves over itself.

Enter the garden?
